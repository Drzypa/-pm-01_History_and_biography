table of common logarithms


This table is for integers in the range -1<n<100. To find the common logarithm (that is, with base 10, the same base of our numeral system) for the desired n, look for the ten’s place digit (0 in the case of -1<n<10) in the leftmost column and the one’s place digit in the topmost row. The intersection x of that row and column thus gives log10⁡n to four or five decimal places.
